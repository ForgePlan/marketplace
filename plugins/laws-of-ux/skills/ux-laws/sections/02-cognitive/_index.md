# Cognitive Laws

Laws and principles related to how users process, store, and retrieve information. These laws address the mental effort required to use interfaces and how cognitive limitations shape design decisions.

## Laws in this section

1. **[Chunking](chunking.md)** -- Breaking information into meaningful groups to aid processing and recall.
2. **[Cognitive Bias](cognitive-bias.md)** -- Systematic errors in thinking that influence perception and decision-making.
3. **[Cognitive Load](cognitive-load.md)** -- The total mental effort required to understand and interact with an interface.
4. **[Miller's Law](millers-law.md)** -- The average person can hold only 7 (plus or minus 2) items in working memory.
5. **[Paradox of the Active User](paradox-of-active-user.md)** -- Users skip manuals and start using software immediately.
6. **[Selective Attention](selective-attention.md)** -- Users focus only on stimuli relevant to their current goals, filtering out everything else.
7. **[Serial Position Effect](serial-position-effect.md)** -- Users best remember the first and last items in a series.
8. **[Von Restorff Effect](von-restorff-effect.md)** -- Among similar objects, the one that differs most is best remembered.
9. **[Working Memory](working-memory.md)** -- A limited cognitive system that temporarily holds and manipulates information needed for tasks.
10. **[Flow](flow.md)** -- The mental state of complete immersion and focused engagement in an activity.
