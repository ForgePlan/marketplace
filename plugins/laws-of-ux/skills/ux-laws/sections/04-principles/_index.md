# Principles

Design principles and behavioral laws that guide how interfaces should be structured to align with human behavior, expectations, and decision-making patterns. These principles provide actionable rules for interaction design, performance, and complexity management.

## Laws in this section

1. **[Doherty Threshold](doherty-threshold.md)** -- Productivity soars when a computer and its users interact at a pace (<400ms) that ensures neither has to wait on the other.
2. **[Goal-Gradient Effect](goal-gradient-effect.md)** -- The tendency to approach a goal increases with proximity to the goal.
3. **[Jakob's Law](jakobs-law.md)** -- Users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know.
4. **[Occam's Razor](occams-razor.md)** -- Among competing hypotheses that predict equally well, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected.
5. **[Pareto Principle](pareto-principle.md)** -- Roughly 80% of the effects come from 20% of the causes.
6. **[Parkinson's Law](parkinsons-law.md)** -- Any task will inflate until all of the available time is spent.
7. **[Peak-End Rule](peak-end-rule.md)** -- People judge an experience largely based on how they felt at its peak and at its end.
8. **[Postel's Law](postels-law.md)** -- Be liberal in what you accept, and conservative in what you send.
9. **[Tesler's Law](teslers-law.md)** -- For any system there is a certain amount of complexity which cannot be reduced.
10. **[Zeigarnik Effect](zeigarnik-effect.md)** -- People remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks.
